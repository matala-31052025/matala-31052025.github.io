General Info

  • Is this a big fat Greek wedding?

    No! The wedding ceremony is taking place a week before with close family only. There’s no fixed schedule — just a beach party!

  • Food? Drinks?

    We’ll have drinks and finger food available throughout the evening.

  • When can I show up?

    We’ll be welcoming guests at Anemi after 20:00. Feel free to arrive in Matala whenever you like!

  • What should I wear?

    It’s a relaxed party on the beach — wear whatever makes you feel comfortable! Late May in Crete is usually mild and pleasant.

  • Gifts?

    We know getting here may be costly. Your presence is more than enough. Really.

  • RSVP?

    Please let us know if you can make it by March 15! Just send a text to Anna or Alex.

Traveling to and staying in Matala

  • How do I get to Crete?

    The easiest way is to fly to Heraklion Airport Nikos Kazantzakis. If you’re in Athens or the Cycladic islands, you can also catch a ferry to Heraklion.

  • How do I get to Matala?

    There are a few options:

    • By car: You can rent a car at the airport. Parking is available in designated areas around Matala.
    • By private transfer: We will arrange for transfer from Heraklion on the day of the party and back on the next. We’ll provide more info on this option soon.
    • By bus: There are line buses (timetables for 2025 to be announced). Stay tuned!

  • Where do I stay? How do I get around Matala?

    Matala is a small, tourist-friendly village right by the sea. We recommend booking accommodation within walking distance of the beach. There are hotels, guesthouses, and even camping options for those who prefer something more outdoorsy.
